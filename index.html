GOVERNMENT OF BERMUDA

Curfew Exemption Form

Please only submit this form once.

This form is online in support of implementing the Public Health (COVID-19 Emergency Powers) (Phased Re-opening) (Amendment (No.7) Regulations 2021. Night-time curfew is in place from 12:30am to 5:00AM.

Form Exemption

You do not need to complete this form if you are travelling for one of the following reasons:

  1. For a Medical Emergency;

  2. To travel to a flight leaving Bermuda;

  3. To drop off or collect someone from the airport;

  4. Parents of a minor child who do not live together may also leave home during the curfew, between the hours of 12:30am to 5:00AM only, to take the child to stay for one or more nights at the home of the other parent to give effect to an agreement between the parents;

  5. Respond to emergency calls during the curfew period for supply, restoration of service or repairs for: utility supply (electricity and domestic gas); water truckers; telecommunications, computer repair and internet service providers; plumbing and electrical repairs.

Exempt Persons

You do not need to complete this form if you are categorized as one of the following persons who are exempt from the curfew only when on official business, working or travelling to and from work, and all such persons shall carry identification at all times:

  1. All members of the House of Assembly and Senate; The staff of the Premier, Ministers and the Opposition Leader; Staff of the Legislature required when the Legislature is sitting

  2. The Deputy Governor and staff of Government House; The US Consul General and staff; Honorary Consuls.

  3. Judges of the Supreme Court, magistrates and court associates and lawyers required for any court proceedings.

  4. The Cabinet Secretary, the Head of the Public Service, the Financial Secretary and Permanent Secretaries.

  5. Members of the Royal Bermuda Regiment, the Bermuda Police Service (including the Reserve Police and Special Constables), the Bermuda Fire and Rescue Service, and the Department of Corrections.

  6. Any registered health professional, ambulance driver or crew, or staff of a healthcare facility (whether employed by the Bermuda Hospitals Board, registered residential care or nursing home, or otherwise), and other staff of the Bermuda Hospitals Board or the Ministry of Health.

  7. Persons working in a hotel, guest house or vacation rental property.

  8. Funeral directors, morticians and other staff of a funeral home.

  9. Members of the Department of Customs and Department of Immigration; all staff employed at the LF Wade International Airport; passengers and crew who need to travel to and from the airport for flights during the curfew, and persons transporting them; stevedores, truckers and staff of Hamilton Docks; licensed security officers.

  10. All persons working in services listed in Schedule 3 to the Trade Union and Labour Relations (Consolidation) Act 2021.

  11. Members of the print or broadcast media.

  12. Members of the Department of Child and Family Services.

  13. Registered commercial farmers and licensed fishermen.

  14. Persons engaged to clean, maintain or repair the premises of any permitted business, or the premises of any business employing persons mentioned in this Schedule.

  15. Public officers not falling within the preceding paragraphs, as designated in writing by the Head of the Public Service, subject to any conditions or limitations included in the designation.

  16. Financial Services

    1. Persons working in the financial services sector, as designated in writing by the Minister of Finance.

    2. A designation made under paragraph 15(1) of Schedule 1 to the Public Health (COVID-19 Emergency Powers) (Stay at Home) Regulations 2021; made under paragraph 12(1) of Schedule 1 to the Public Health (COVID-19 Emergency Powers) Regulations 2021 or paragraph 13(1) of Schedule 1A to those Regulations; made under paragraph 12(1) of Schedule 1 to the Public Health (COVID-19 Emergency Powers) (No. 3) Regulations 2020; made under, or that was deemed to be valid for the purposes of, paragraph 11(1) of Schedule 1 to the Public Health (COVID-19 Emergency Powers) Regulations 2020; or made under, or that was deemed to be valid for the purposes of, paragraph 15(1) of Schedule 1 to these Regulations (before revocation), shall be deemed to be valid for the purposes of subparagraph (1).

Persons with exemptions who are in public must have a copy of Valid Driving License and or any Government issued Valid ID and confirmation of your place of employment (Letter or Work ID).
